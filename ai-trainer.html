The Process

Five Steps to a Fully Custom Program

This approach is built around a simple idea: the more we know about your body before session one, the better every session becomes. This process replaces the traditional "get-to-know-you" consultation with a thorough, AI-assisted intake that Edgar reviews personally.

01

Complete Your Health Screening

You'll complete the full 2023 PAR-Q+ Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire ‚Äî the same clinical standard used by certified fitness professionals and medical providers. Any flags are reviewed by Edgar before programming begins.

PAR-Q+ ¬∑ Medical Clearance
02

Fill Out Your Lifestyle & Health History

A comprehensive questionnaire covering your exercise history, diet, sleep, stress, occupation, past injuries, and surgeries. This is the same NASM CPT7 Lifestyle and Health History form used in professional settings ‚Äî your answers directly inform your corrective exercise protocol.

NASM CPT7 Standard
03

Self-Report Your Body Composition

Height, weight, circumference measurements, resting heart rate, and blood pressure (if known). BMI and Waist-to-Hip Ratio are calculated automatically. This data establishes your baseline and helps Edgar track measurable progress over time.

Body Composition Baseline
04

Submit Your Movement Assessment Videos

Record and upload short videos of your Overhead Squat (OHSA), Single-Leg Squat, Pushing, Pulling, and Cardio assessments. Edgar uses these to identify movement compensations ‚Äî things like knee valgus, excessive forward lean, or shoulder elevation ‚Äî and maps them directly to your corrective exercise plan using the NASM biomechanics framework.

OHSA ¬∑ SLSA ¬∑ Push/Pull ¬∑ Cardio
05

Edgar Analyzes, Then Builds Your Program

Once submitted, Edgar reviews an AI-generated pre-session briefing covering your risk flags, biomechanical findings, corrective exercise priorities, and behavior stage. He then writes your fully custom program: warm-up, corrective protocol, strength progression, and cardio prescription ‚Äî all based on your specific body mechanics and goals.
